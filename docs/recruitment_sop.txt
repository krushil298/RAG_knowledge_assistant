PATIENT RECRUITMENT AND SCREENING STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP-204)

Purpose
This SOP defines the process for identifying, pre-screening, and enrolling
participants into clinical trials conducted at research sites. It applies to all
Phase 1 through Phase 4 studies.

Pre-Screening
Potential participants are first identified through the patient database, community
outreach, and referrals. A recruitment specialist conducts an initial pre-screen by
phone using the study-specific pre-screening questionnaire. The pre-screen must be
completed within 48 hours of the participant's first contact.

Eligibility Window
Once a participant passes pre-screening, the formal screening visit must be scheduled
within 14 days. This 14-day period is referred to as the screening window. If the
window is missed, the participant must be re-pre-screened before a screening visit can
be booked.

Informed Consent
Informed consent must be obtained before any study-specific procedures are performed.
The consenting process must be conducted by a delegated and trained staff member, and
the signed consent form must be stored in the participant's regulatory file. A copy is
provided to the participant.

Data Quality
All source data must be recorded contemporaneously and must be attributable, legible,
contemporaneous, original, and accurate (the ALCOA principles). Any correction to source
data must be made with a single line strike-through, initialed, and dated. Correction
fluid is prohibited.

Adverse Events
Any adverse event reported by a participant must be documented within 24 hours and
escalated to the Principal Investigator. Serious adverse events (SAEs) must be reported
to the sponsor within 24 hours of site awareness.

Retention
Sites are expected to maintain a participant retention rate above 85 percent for the
duration of a study. Missed visits must be followed up within 3 business days.
